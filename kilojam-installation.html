Description:

KILO-JAM is an interactive visual and sound performance installation created by the BANG! collective.

The installation consists of a series of gym equipment (2 punching bags, 1 stationary bike, and a step) converted into musical instruments using sensors and computers. The interactors are encouraged to interact with the instruments to produce a musical "jam" as synchronized as possible to evolve the computer-generated music and transition it from trip-hop to electro and finally to drum and bass. The evolution of the jam is visible on a projection that indicates the level of synchronicity achieved. A wall of bare screens mounted in an ABS structure broadcasts videos that react to the interactions of the instruments in the manner of a Video Jockey.

KILO-JAM aims to break down the barriers of art and seeks to integrate public space and people's daily lives. This installation was presented twice in 2006. First, at the agora of the Judith-Jasmin Pavilion of the University of Quebec in Montreal on the occasion of the Agora Festif 03. Then, the installation was presented at the Alizé bar-concert hall in Montreal with the collaboration of DJ Barham and DJ Marlen Blondeau.
